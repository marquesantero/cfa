CFA is entering a focused cycle. The next release (`0.2.0`) is editorial,
not feature work — we are removing what dilutes the core idea and tightening
what makes CFA distinctive.

<!-- truncate -->

## What is changing

- **No more framework adapter shims.** `cfa.adapters.langgraph`, `crewai`,
  `autogen`, `dspy`, and `openai_agents` were aliases of the same
  `cfa_guard` decorator. They are gone in `0.2.0`. Use `cfa.adapters.cfa_guard`
  directly. The new
  [Use cfa_guard with any framework](/docs/integrations/use-cfa-guard-with-frameworks)
  guide shows the pattern for LangGraph, CrewAI, AutoGen, DSPy, and OpenAI
  Agents SDK.
- **Package consolidation.** 20 subpackages → ~13. Old names keep working
  with a `DeprecationWarning` for two minor releases.
- **Site rewrite.** Operational language. Status banner. Comparison page
  against OPA, LangSmith, Great Expectations, and Unity Catalog.
- **Architectural Decision Records.** Every significant decision now lives
  in `docs/adr/` as a small, dated document.

## What is not changing

The five primitives that make CFA distinctive stay exactly as they are:

1. Typed, content-hashed `StateSignature`.
2. `REPLAN` as a first-class outcome alongside `APPROVE` and `BLOCK`.
3. Offline-verifiable SHA-256 audit chain.
4. Operational catalog (PII, partition, classification, merge_key as policy
   primitives, not just metadata).
5. Deterministic by default; LLM as an optional normalizer plug-in.

## What comes next

`0.3.0` introduces the first real integration — `cfa dbt check`, a
pre-`dbt run` gate that derives a `StateSignature` for each model from
`target/manifest.json` and runs the policy bundle against it.

Full plan in [`drafts/ROADMAP.md`](https://github.com/marquesantero/cfa/blob/main/drafts/ROADMAP.md).
